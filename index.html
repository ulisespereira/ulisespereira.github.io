About me

I am fifth year PhD student at the Department of Statistics at the University of Chicago. Advised by Nicolas Brunel. My research is centered on building theoretical models for understanding how neural representations are learned from experience and used for cognitive processes in brain systems. Using a combination of mechanistic modeling and normative approaches from optimization principles, I build theories for understanding neural representations in the brain. I put special emphasis in constraining such theories by recordings of populations of neurons.

Previously, I studied at the Universidad de Chile, where I completed a Bachelor's degree in Physics (2012), an Engineer's degree in Biotechnology (2013), and a Master's degree in Physics (2013) with specialization in nonlinear dynamics.
